hollywood to hackney...

WordsGuest Writer
Issue02 / Summer
Read18 Min
FiledMusic / DJs

London summer is a rumor — grey mornings, basement sets, a DJ you only catch at 2am when the tube has stopped running. Los Angeles summer is the proof: pool decks, dry air, someone else's playlist bleeding through a sliding door at noon. I live between both and trust neither completely.

In Hackney the music starts late and close. Low ceilings, fogged windows, a friend of a friend on the decks playing UK garage off a laptop that skips when someone opens the fire door. You dance because the room is small and the bass is physical — not metaphorical, not playlist-curated, just weight in your chest until the sun comes up wrong.

In LA the same season arrives as heat. The DJ is outside now — rooftop, backyard, a hotel pool you weren't invited to but walked into anyway. The set is longer, looser, Britney into Glass into something a local producer sent at 3am with no title. Nobody checks the time. The sun stays up like it owes you money. London feels like memory; Los Angeles feels like now.

I keep both on one phone. Voice memos from a pirate radio show taped off a window in Dalston. A SoundCloud link from a DJ in Highland Park who only plays when the marine layer burns off. The gap between them is eight hours and one life — same summer canon, different volume. You learn cities through who plays what, when, and how loud they'll let it get.

What transfers is not the tracklist — it's the posture. Head down in London. Shoulders back in LA. Both are forms of listening. Both assume the night will continue. A DJ in either city is really a weather report: this is how hot it feels, this is how long you have, this is what we're calling summer tonight.

"Same season, different volume — London in your chest, LA on your skin."
— between sets, issue 02

People ask which city I prefer in summer. I say both, which is a DJ answer — not a refusal, a blend. The best nights I remember are crossfaded: garage from a London basement still in my ears while a Hollywood pool deck plays something slower, sweeter, meant for daylight. Music makes the distance smaller. You can hold Hackney at noon in Pasadena if the right record is on.

Maybe that is the essay — not arrival, not departure, just two summers running at once in one playlist, one body, one season that refuses to pick a coast.

I am not trying to resolve London and Los Angeles. I am trying to DJ between them — A side, B side, no clear winner, just a mix that makes sense at the time. Summer Pop on the index. UK garage in the group chat. A set list saved under two names because the cities share songs but never the same sky.

Take care of yourself. The DJ says it without speaking — every transition a small negotiation, every drop a bet that the room will follow. London stays in the bass. LA stays in the light. The playlist stays open. I have never chosen one summer. I have always been listening.

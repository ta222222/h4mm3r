Summer Pop

Britney, Glass, pool decks — procession music for the season that never ends. Drag the looks. Cut them out. Make them yours.

I do not write essays the way editors expect. I write in tape, sunscreen smears, and the order you stack references before a fitting. Summer Pop is not a genre — it is a temperature. Britney on a cracked speaker. Glass when the light goes pink. A playlist that assumes you are already wet.

Every season I build a board like this one and send it to musicians who never asked. They reply with a single emoji or a voice note from a cab. That is collaboration in 2026. Drag the images if the layout insults you. Polka-dot the ones that feel too serious. Cut out the ones that look like they belong to someone else's memory.

"Procession music for the season that never ends — styled, not explained."

The stylist's job is to make desire legible without killing it. Pool decks. Procession routes. Pop as liturgy. If you need a thesis, close the tab. If you need a mood, you are already in the right humidity.

Nova Delcourt Guest stylist for Issue 02. Former runway dresser, current mood-board evangelist. Based between London heatwaves and LA pool hours.
